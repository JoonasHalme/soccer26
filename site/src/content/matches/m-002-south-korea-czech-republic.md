The night-cap of the opening day. Group A's second pair plays at Estadio Akron in Guadalajara, on neutral ground for both — neither team gets the venue boost.

**South Korea** are the second seed of Group A and one of Asia's two real heavyweights at this point. They built around Son Heung-min for the better part of a decade and the cycle that produced this squad has emphasised press-resistance and quick vertical play — fewer crosses, more half-space combinations. Their AFC qualifying record this cycle was excellent, which the Elo (~1716) captures.

**Czech Republic** are the dark horse profile — never the favourites, never embarrassed, always organised. The block is mid-low, the transitions go through whoever the in-form striker is, and they're disciplined enough on dead balls to keep tight matches even-money for stretches. The Elo (~1626) probably understates them on a knockout-stage day; group stage is where they can sleepwalk a little.

**Model view.** Korea **54% / 25% / 21%**, xG **1.63–0.92**, O2.5 47%, BTTS 48%. Tight but Korea-leaning. Without the home boost (neutral venue), the gap is pure quality differential.

- **Czech +0.5 Asian handicap** if priced above ~1.85 (combined draw + Czech win = 46%, fair odds ~2.17)
