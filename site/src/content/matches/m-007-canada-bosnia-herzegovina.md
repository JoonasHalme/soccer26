Canada's opener at BMO Field in Toronto, second game of the tournament. The hosts get a winnable spot to start, the kind of fixture you have to bank when you're a co-host trying to build momentum through a group.

**Canada** qualified automatically as a host nation, so like Mexico the friendlies-vs-competitive trade-off applies. The squad's identity has been built around speed in transition (Davies, David), with a possession base that has improved match by match through the cycle. They flopped at Qatar 2022 with zero points; the project is now four years older.

**Bosnia & Herzegovina** are the awkward draw the Elo system makes look easy. Rating ~1452 reflects a brutal stretch since 2020 — failed qualifications, generational handover from the Pjanić/Džeko era — and may overstate the gap to a Canada side they would historically be much closer to. They concede a lot in friendlies and look listless in dead rubbers, then suddenly turn out a coherent performance in a competitive opener.
